What are personality disorders?

Personality disorders are types of mental disorders in which a person’s behavior, thoughts, and attitude may cause problems in life. Someone with a personality disorder will often have difficulty relating to others.

There are ten personality disorders, which are divided into three clusters. The first cluster (cluster A) contains disorders that make it difficult to relate to others. The disorders in this group are paranoid personality disorder, schizoid personality disorder, and schizotypal personality disorder.

The second cluster (cluster B) refers to overly dramatic and emotional thinking, or unpredictable behavior. This includes antisocial personality disorder, borderline personality disorder, histrionic personality disorder, and narcissistic personality disorder. Someone diagnosed with one of these disorders might find it hard to control their emotions.

The third cluster (cluster C) describes fearful or anxious behavior such as avoidant personality disorder, dependent personality disorder, and obsessive-compulsive personality disorder.

Cluster A

Paranoid Personality Disorder

One of the cluster A personality disorders is paranoid personality disorder. Someone diagnosed with this condition is constantly suspicious of other people, with the belief that everyone is trying to harm them. Because of their general distrust and paranoia, it is difficult to form close relationships with them.

Some symptoms are:

  1. Constant suspicion of other people and their motives
  2. Doubts the loyalty and trustworthiness of other people
  3. Reluctant to confide in others due to fear that the information will be used against you
  4. Holds grudges
  5. Thinks of innocent remarks as personal insults

Schizoid Personality Disorder

Schizoid personality disorder is a mental condition in which people avoid social interactions and activities. People with schizoid personality disorder tend to show limited emotion and are often portrayed as a loner, because of their inability and lack of desire for close relationships.

A few symptoms include:

  1. A preference to being alone
  2. Appears cold or indifferent to others
  3. Little to none desire for sexual relationships
  4. Inability to feel pleasure
  5. Limited range of showing emotion

Schizotypal Personality Disorder

Schizotypal personality disorder is a type of personality disorder that’s part of cluster A, with the definition of bizarre and odd. People with schizotypal personality disorder don’t understand how relationships work, and generally have social anxiety, with only a few close relationships. They usually misinterpret other peoples intentions and behavior, leading to suspicion or distrust.

A few symptoms include:

  1. Belief that harmless events have hidden meanings
  2. Unusual and odd dress, beliefs, speech, or behavior
  3. Social anxiety and paranoia
  4. Doubts on other people's loyalty
  5. Beliieving in superpowers
  6. Limited emotional responses

Schizotypal personality disorder is a part of the schizophrenia spectrum and other psychotic disorders group. While one with schizotypal personality disorder might experience psychotic episodes similar to schizophrenia, they are not as severe

Cluster B

Antisocial Personality Disorder

Similar to sociopathy, antisocial personality disorder is a mental illness where a person shows no care or emotion towards other people. People with antisocial personality disorder tend to act impulsively, which is why most of them end up breaking the law. They won’t hesitate to use others for their own gains, and cease to feel remorse for their behavior.

A few common symptoms include:

  1. Pays no attention to right or wrong
  2. Treats others with disrespect
  3. Acts impulsively
  4. Lying and manipulating others for personal gains

Borderline Personality Disorder

Borderline personality disorder is a mental disorder where the person affected has quick changes in emotion, their self, and other people. Someone with this condition will have an intense fear of being abandoned and unable to tolerate being alone. They tend to have a short temper and risky behavior.

Someone with this condition will have symptoms such as:

  1. An excessive fear of abandonment
  2. Rapid changes in self-identity
  3. Intense emotions that last from a few hours to days, but change quickly
  4. Easily angered
  5. A difficulty to hold in rage
  6. Suicidal behavior or thoughts

Histronic Personality Disorder

Histrionic personality disorder is a mental disorder, in which the affected person is constantly seeking attention, and often behave dramatically or inappropriately to be noticed. They depend on the approval of other people, and are only comfortable when they are in the spotlight.

A couple symptoms are:

  1. Always seeking attention
  2. Very dramatic and emotional to get noticed
  3. Easily influenced by others
  4. Concern over physical appearance

Narcissistic Personality Disorder

It's good to have confidence in yourself. But, is it possible to have too much? Narcissistic personality disorder is a mental condition where people would constantly have a mindset that they are more superior than everyone else, and require an excessive amount of praise. Because of their self-centeredness, someone with narcissistic personality disorder would lack empathy for other people.

Even if they act full of self-confidence, they are easily hurt by criticism and may have negative thoughts about themselves inside.

Some symptoms include:

  1. Constant belief that they are superior
  2. Often exaggerates achievments
  3. Unable or unwilling to notice other people's needs
  4. Believes that others envy them or envys others
  5. Arrogant
  6. Envisions fantasies about success

Cluster C

Avoidant Personality Disorder

Many find it difficult to interact with others. But if a socially awkward person had a huge sensitivity to criticism and constant feelings of inferiority, then they may have a mental condition called avoidant personality disorder. Someone with avoidant personality disorder will often avoid social interactions, due to fear of criticism, embarrassment, or rejection. They tend to have low self-esteem, and are especially sensitive to being criticized or rejected.

Someone diagnosed with this condition will:

  1. Avoid social activities for fear of criticism or rejection
  2. Be unwilling to interact with others unless sure of a positive response
  3. Feel sensitive to criticism and rejection
  4. See self as inferior and unappealing
  5. Be reluctant to take risks or try new things due to fear of embarassing self

Dependent Personality Disorder

Its good to depend on others for assistance, but if someone is overdependent on other people, they might have dependent personality disorder. Someone with dependent personality disorder will find it hard to make decisions for themselves, and often relies on other close figures to make decisions for them. They generally display clingy and passive behavior, and greatly fear separation from a close one.

Some common symptoms include:

  1. Unable to make decisions without help
  2. Fear of being alone or having to take care of self
  3. Hard to disagree with others, for fear of losing support
  4. Inability to start or do projects due to lack of self-confidence
  5. Depends on others to make decisions for them
  6. Quickly finds a new relationship after a close on ends

Obsessive-Compulsive Personality Disorder

Obsessive-compulsive personality disorder (OCPD) falls into cluster C of the personality disorders. People with OCPD generally think that their way of doing things is the right way and follow a strict routine in their daily life even if the routines aren’t efficient. They try to enforce their ways of living onto other people and become distressed when they are unable to keep order. They are preoccupied by orderliness and perfection, at the expense of flexibility and efficiency.

A few symptoms are:

  1. Preoccupied with details, orderliness, and rules
  2. Interference with task completion because of perfectionism
  3. Unable to discard worthless or broken objects
  4. Stubborn and rigid
  5. Reluctant to work with others unless sure they will follow their lead

This is often confused with obsessive-compulsive disorder, but though they are similar, they are two separate conditions.
